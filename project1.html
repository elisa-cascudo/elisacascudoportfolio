Customer Segmentation Analysis

Using machine learning to identify distinct customer groups and drive targeted marketing strategies

Python K-Means Clustering Tableau Pandas Scikit-learn

üìã Project Overview

This project was undertaken for a mid-sized e-commerce company struggling with one-size-fits-all marketing campaigns that were yielding diminishing returns. The goal was to segment their customer base into meaningful groups to enable personalized marketing strategies.

üéØ Objectives

  • Identify distinct customer segments based on purchasing behavior
  • Develop actionable insights for targeted marketing
  • Create an automated pipeline for ongoing segmentation
  • Measure impact on marketing efficiency and ROI
50K+ Customers Analyzed

üîç The Challenge

The company had accumulated 3 years of customer transaction data but wasn't leveraging it effectively. Their marketing team was sending the same campaigns to all customers, resulting in:

  • Low email open rates (averaging 12%)
  • Declining customer engagement
  • Wasted marketing budget on irrelevant campaigns
  • No clear understanding of different customer types

They needed a data-driven approach to understand their customers better and tailor their marketing accordingly.

üí° Approach & Methodology

1. Data Collection & Preparation

I started by gathering and cleaning customer transaction data from their database:

  • Transaction history (50,000+ customers over 3 years)
  • Purchase frequency and recency
  • Average order value
  • Product categories purchased
  • Customer lifetime value
import pandas as pd import numpy as np from sklearn.preprocessing import StandardScaler from sklearn.cluster import KMeans import matplotlib.pyplot as plt import seaborn as sns # Load and preprocess data df = pd.read_csv('customer_transactions.csv') # Feature engineering df['recency'] = (df['last_purchase_date'] - df['first_purchase_date']).dt.days df['frequency'] = df['total_orders'] df['monetary'] = df['total_spent'] # Create RFM features features = df[['recency', 'frequency', 'monetary']] scaler = StandardScaler() features_scaled = scaler.fit_transform(features)

3. Model Selection & Training

I used K-Means clustering after testing multiple algorithms. The elbow method and silhouette analysis helped determine the optimal number of clusters (k=5):

# Determine optimal k using elbow method inertias = [] silhouette_scores = [] for k in range(2, 11): kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=k, random_state=42, n_init=10) kmeans.fit(features_scaled) inertias.append(kmeans.inertia_) silhouette_scores.append(silhouette_score(features_scaled, kmeans.labels_)) # Train final model with k=5 final_model = KMeans(n_clusters=5, random_state=42, n_init=10) df['cluster'] = final_model.fit_predict(features_scaled)

4. Segment Analysis & Profiling

After clustering, I analyzed each segment to create actionable profiles:

  • VIP Champions (12%): High value, frequent buyers - target with premium offers
  • Loyal Customers (28%): Regular purchases, medium value - nurture with loyalty programs
  • Potential Loyalists (24%): Recent customers with potential - engage with personalized recommendations
  • At Risk (21%): Previously active, now declining - re-engagement campaigns needed
  • Lost Customers (15%): Haven't purchased in 6+ months - win-back campaigns

üìä Results & Impact

Key Achievements:

  • 25% increase in marketing efficiency: By targeting campaigns to specific segments
  • Email open rates improved from 12% to 31%: Thanks to personalized messaging
  • 18% revenue growth: From better customer retention and upselling
  • 40% reduction in churn: Through proactive re-engagement of at-risk customers
  • Automated pipeline: Weekly updates ensure segments stay current

The marketing team now runs segment-specific campaigns:

üí≠ Key Learnings

  • RFM analysis is powerful: Recency, Frequency, and Monetary value provide excellent segmentation features
  • Business context matters: Working closely with the marketing team ensured segments were actionable
  • Automation is crucial: An automated pipeline ensures insights stay relevant
  • Visualization helps adoption: Clear Tableau dashboards made it easy for stakeholders to understand and use the segments
  • Start simple: K-Means was sufficient; more complex algorithms didn't improve results significantly
